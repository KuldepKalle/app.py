ICT Kill Zone Strategy for XAUUSD
🕐 LONDON KILL ZONE (IST: 11:30 AM – 2:30 PM)
The best gold setup window. London KZ (2:00–5:00 AM EDT = 11:30 AM–2:30 PM IST) typically establishes the day's high or low by running Asian session highs/lows (Judas Swing) to hunt retail stops. After the fake-out, the real daily direction begins. Strategy: Mark Asian High/Low (built 4:30–9:30 AM IST) → Wait for price to sweep it early in London KZ → Look for rejection + 5M FVG → Enter at OTE (70.5% retrace) with SL beyond the sweep point.
🕐 NEW YORK KILL ZONE (IST: 4:30 PM – 7:30 PM)
NY KZ (7:00–10:00 AM EDT = 4:30–7:30 PM IST) usually continues or confirms the London direction. The NY/London overlap (IST 6:00–7:30 PM) is the single highest-probability window for XAUUSD — gold's daily range is set ~70% of the time here. Often creates the daily extreme. Judas Swing here hunts London session highs/lows. Enter on retracements to 50% or OTE of London expansion.
⭐ SILVER BULLET (IST: 7:30 PM – 8:30 PM)
ICT's highest-probability 60-minute window for gold (10:00–11:00 AM EDT = 7:30–8:30 PM IST). Look for a 1M or 5M FVG forming after a liquidity sweep. This captures the final New York institutional move of the day before the dead zone. Entry: Wait for displacement candle → identify FVG → enter on retest of FVG with SL beyond sweep point.
❌ DEAD ZONE — AVOID (IST: 9:00 PM – 10:30 PM)
NY Lunch time (11:30 AM–1:00 PM EDT = 9:00–10:30 PM IST). Low probability, often reversal traps. No institutional order flow. Price moves are thin and fake. Rule: If you're not in a position by 8:30 PM IST (end of Silver Bullet), wait for the next day.
Power of 3 (AMD) — How Institutions Structure Each Day
PHASE 1
ACCUMULATION
Asian session (4:30–9:30 AM IST). Tight range. Mark the High and Low — these are your key levels for the Judas Swing.
PHASE 2
MANIPULATION
London KZ (11:30 AM–2:30 PM IST). Fake breakout hunts stops beyond Asian H/L. This is the Judas Swing. Wait for the reversal — do NOT trade the fake move.
PHASE 3
DISTRIBUTION
NY KZ + Silver Bullet (4:30–8:30 PM IST). The real move. Enter after manipulation confirms. Target opposing liquidity (PDH/PDL).

Why Your Signals Hit SL — Root Cause Analysis
⚠ TODAY'S MARKET CONDITION — WHY SL HITS ARE HIGH
Gold (XAUUSD) is currently in a tight range consolidation between $4,600–$4,700 with no clear directional bias. The daily candle shows a doji pattern — indecision. In ranging/choppy markets, trend-following setups fail frequently because price reverses before reaching TP.
5 Root Causes of Your SL Hits
① SL TOO TIGHT — 14 pips is not enough for gold
XAUUSD has an average intraday range of 200–500 pips per session. A 14-pip SL (= $0.14 move) is smaller than a single spread-plus-wick on most brokers. Gold routinely wicks 20–40 pips before moving in the intended direction.
Fix: Minimum SL for gold should be 20–30 pips (based on OB size + 5-pip buffer). Ideally place SL below/above the 30M Order Block, not a fixed distance.
② RANGING MARKET — signals need a TREND filter
Today gold is stuck in a $4,600–$4,700 range (100-pip range = choppy). The dashboard fires signals based on short-term momentum, but in a ranging market, price bounces between support and resistance without trending. Signals that look like BOS/CHoCH are just noise inside the range.
Fix: Added ADR Filter below. If the day's current range is less than 40% of the 5-day average range, signals should be treated as C-grade only.
③ ENTRY TOO EARLY — not waiting for 5M/1M confirmation
The signal fires when price reaches the OB zone, but the actual entry should only be taken after a 5M or 1M rejection candle (pin bar, engulfing) CLOSES inside the zone. If you entered at the moment the signal fired without waiting for candle confirmation, you entered into an OB that price could continue through.
Fix: Always wait for a 1M or 5M candle to CLOSE with rejection before entering. The signal is an alert to watch, not an instant entry.
④ TOO MANY SIGNALS — overtrading in same session
If you received many A-grade signals today, the algorithm was firing too frequently due to the 60-second check interval. In choppy conditions, price oscillates across OB zones repeatedly, triggering multiple signals in the same range. More signals ≠ better signals.
Fix: Signal cooldown increased to 4 hours per direction. Max 2 signals per session direction. A+ only during London+NY overlap.
⑤ NO DAILY BIAS FILTER — trading against the daily candle
If the daily candle is bearish (close below open) but intraday signals were firing BUY setups, you were trading against the higher timeframe bias. Today's daily candle is a doji — neither bull nor bear — so both directions carry equal risk.
Fix: Added daily candle filter. Only BUY signals when daily close > open. Only SELL signals when daily close < open. Doji days = reduced signal quality grade.
